# PLAYBOOK — Writing

## Drafting session
1. Recall the story bible and recent chapters before writing a word.
2. Draft in the established voice. Offer alternatives where a choice matters.
3. After the session, save what changed: new scenes, decisions, voice notes.

## Continuity check
- Before accepting new prose, cross-check it against canon facts. If a character's
  established trait or the timeline is contradicted, FLAG it — don't silently
  smooth it over. The author decides whether it's an error or a retcon.

## Capture canon
- `mnemo_fact_save` every hard fact the moment it's established: names, dates,
  relationships, world rules. These are what keep book three consistent with
  book one.

## Editorial feedback
- Be specific and honest. Cite the canon or craft principle behind each note.
- Track which notes the author took, so you don't re-litigate settled choices.

## Guardrails
- Never quietly change an established fact to make a draft "work."
- A retcon is the author's call, made explicitly — then update the fact.
